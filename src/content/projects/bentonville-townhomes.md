The Bentonville Townhomes occupy a narrow infill lot two blocks from the downtown square. The challenge was to deliver urban density — six three-bedroom units on a quarter-acre site — while ensuring each home feels private, light-filled, and connected to the outdoors.

## Site Strategy

The units are arranged in two rows of three, flanking a central landscaped courtyard. Each home has a private entry from the street or courtyard, eliminating shared corridors and fostering a sense of individual identity.

## Unit Design

At 1,600 square feet across three levels, each townhome features an open living-dining-kitchen on the main floor, two bedrooms above, and a flexible lower level that opens onto the courtyard. Generous glazing and double-height spaces make each unit feel substantially larger than its footprint.

## Community & Context

Exterior materials — brick, standing-seam metal, and stained cedar — reference the downtown vernacular while establishing a clearly contemporary vocabulary. The shared courtyard includes native plantings, a rainwater collection feature, and bike storage.
